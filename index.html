Abstract

In real-world deployment, vision-language models often encounter disturbances such as weather, occlusion, and camera motion. Under such conditions, their understanding and reasoning degrade substantially, revealing a gap between clean, controlled evaluation settings and real-world robustness. To address this limitation, we propose ROVA, a novel training framework that improves robustness by modeling a robustness-aware consistency reward under spatio-temporal corruptions. ROVA introduces a difficulty-aware online training strategy that prioritizes informative samples based on the model's evolving capability. Specifically, it continuously re-estimates sample difficulty via self-reflective evaluation, enabling adaptive training with a robustness-aware consistency reward. We also introduce PVRBench, a new benchmark that injects real-world perturbations into embodied video datasets to assess both accuracy and reasoning quality under realistic disturbances. We evaluate ROVA and baselines on PVRBench, UrbanVideo, and VisBench, where open-source and proprietary models suffer up to 35% and 28% drops in accuracy and reasoning under realistic perturbations. ROVA effectively mitigates performance degradation, boosting relative accuracy by at least 24% and reasoning by over 9% compared with baseline models (QWen2.5/3-VL, InternVL2.5, Embodied-R). These gains transfer to clean standard benchmarks, yielding consistent improvements.

ROVA: Robust Video Alignment

Figure 2: ROVA consists of (1) structured spatio-temporal corruption, (2) self-reflective difficulty-aware training, and (3) dual-branch alignment with GRPO.

1. Structured Spatio-Temporal Corruption

We apply realistic perturbations (weather, lighting, occlusion, camera motion) with spatial grounding and temporal coherence. Each video is paired with its corrupted counterpart for dual-branch alignment.

2. Self‑Reflective Difficulty‑Aware Training

The model evaluates its own confidence and difficulty on corrupted samples. Easy samples are discarded, hard ones stored in a memory buffer for later replay, and informative ones used for training. This forms an adaptive curriculum that evolves with the model.

3. Dual‑Branch Alignment with GRPO

We enforce output consistency between clean and perturbed branches using a reward composed of format, accuracy, and alignment rewards (reasoning + answer similarity). Optimization is done via Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO).

PVRBench: Perturbed Video Reasoning Benchmark

PVRBench systematically injects 12 realistic perturbation types (lighting: dusk, night, overexposure, shadow; camera: translation, zoom, rotation; occlusion: static, dynamic; weather: fog, rain, snow) into 9K videos from UrbanVideo and VSI-Bench, creating 51K QA pairs. All perturbations are spatially aware and temporally coherent.
